1. Executive Summary

Hunter Galloway engaged Conversion Rate Store (CRS / conversionrate.store) in approximately March 2024 for website CRO (conversion rate optimisation), development, and later Meta ads management, at a retainer of approximately $9,000 per month.

Over 25 months, CRS:

  • Shipped a redesign that collapsed conversion rates by 37% and cost $60k with a 30% revenue drop (Jun 2024)
  • "Fixed" a bug that broke the contact form — Jayden had to roll back the server himself (Aug 2024)
  • Built a suburb dropdown that listed the wrong data and did nothing when clicked (Apr 2024)
  • Shipped forms that stored email addresses as first names in HubSpot — 90 contacts corrupted (Oct 2025)
  • Hardcoded dashes instead of quiz answers for 167 contacts (Dec 2025)
  • Never connected the refinance quiz webhook — 80+ leads lost (discovered Apr 2026)
  • Denied knowledge of Zapier despite their own team verifying it in writing 16 months earlier
  • Told Jayden "QA issues resolved" on 3 March 2026 while 28 leads were corrupted that same month
  • Promised React site live by 7 April 2026 — not delivered
  • Provided zero test cases, zero test plans, zero test reports across 201 emails and the entire engagement

Estimated revenue lost from the form corruption alone: $53,032 – $93,158. This excludes the $60k redesign damage, lost leads from the broken contact form, and the undelivered React migration.

Lie #1: "First time we've heard of Zapier"

Gleb Hodorovskiy, 2 April 2026: "If we had known about the Zapier automation... it's the first time we've heard"

Ihor Sokolov (CRS team lead), 19 July 2024: "We have verified that the conversions from the previous version are being streamed to Zapier and the following email addresses: ... 9wymd7m4@robot.zapier.com"

CRS verified the exact Zapier webhook address 20 months before claiming ignorance. This is not a mistake in memory — it is a documented, searchable email from their own team lead.

Update (3 Apr 2026): When confronted with Ihor's July 2024 email, Gleb shifted from "first time we've heard" to: "I'm copied on a large number of emails and, regrettably, I missed that specific one." The claim moved from "we didn't know" to "I personally missed it" — but CRS is an organisation, not one person. Ihor verified the webhook as part of CRS's deployment process. Institutional knowledge doesn't disappear because the co-founder didn't read the email.

Lie #2: "We've already resolved the QA issues"

Gleb Hodorovskiy, 3 March 2026 (in response to Jayden proposing to reduce scope)

March 2026: 28 contacts with email stored as first name — the worst month on record. The homepage quiz was shipping hardcoded dashes for every submission. Test data "Test1@Gmail.Com" was left in production HubSpot.

This statement was made to convince Jayden to continue paying the full $9k/month retainer. Jayden continued based on this assurance.

Lie #3: "All leads captured correctly... issue is on your side"

Yuliia Boiko, 1 April 2026: "All leads submitted through the form have been consistently captured in HubSpot... This leads us to believe the issue is not with lead capture, but rather with how leads were being monitored or processed on your side."

90 contacts had email as firstname. 167 had all quiz fields hardcoded to "-". 15 had zero quiz data. 72+ form instances missing transaction_type. The JavaScript literally reads: r.append("mortgage-rate", "-")

CRS attributed the problem to Hunter Galloway's monitoring before investigating their own code. "Captured in HubSpot" is technically true in the way "the house is still standing" is true after the plumbing floods every room.

How the bugs went undetected for 6 months

CRS's QA process checked:

  • Whether the form rendered on the page (visual check)
  • Whether clicking Submit produced a "Thank you" message (UI check)
  • Whether submission volume increased (A/B test metrics)

CRS's QA process never checked:

  • Whether the submitted data arrived with correct field mappings in HubSpot
  • Whether the firstname field contained a name (not an email address)
  • Whether the quiz answer fields contained answers (not dashes)
  • Whether the webhook was connected

CRS's own test contacts proved the bug was visible from day one:

11. Verdict

The Record Speaks for Itself

Over 25 months, Hunter Galloway paid CRS approximately $225,000. In return:

• A redesign that collapsed conversions by 37% and caused a 30% revenue drop

• A contact form that CRS broke and Jayden had to fix

• A suburb dropdown that displayed the wrong data and did nothing

• Quiz forms that corrupted 90 contacts, hardcoded dashes for 167 more, and lost 80+ leads entirely

• A React migration that was never delivered

• Meta ads with an 80% no-answer rate

• Three provable lies in writing when confronted

• Zero test cases across the entire engagement

Every time something broke, the client fixed it or discovered it months later. Every time the client tried to leave, CRS made promises that were not kept. Every time CRS was confronted, they either denied knowledge, shifted blame to the client, or minimised the damage.

Estimated total damage: $53,032 – $93,158 in lost revenue from form corruption alone. This excludes the redesign disaster, the broken contact form, the wasted Meta ad spend, and the undelivered React migration.

All evidence is independently verifiable via HubSpot CRM API (Account 4607234), Gmail search, and Wayback Machine snapshots. All email citations are from CRS's own communications.

Why the Blame-Shift Fails

  • CRS's own test contacts (Test6677@Mail.Com, 10 Oct 2025) already showed the bug. CRS didn't catch it in their own test data — monitoring is not the client's substitute for QA.
  • Gleb committed to "no item marked ready without production validation" on 23 Dec 2025. Production validation means CRS verifies their own deployments.
  • The bug is not a subtle data formatting issue visible only in HubSpot. It's r.append("mortgage-rate", "-") — hardcoded in CRS's JavaScript. And the email-as-name mapping is a CF7 configuration error, not a monitoring failure.
  • CRS verified Zapier E2E delivery in Jul 2024, establishing end-to-end verification as their own standard. They then stopped doing it for subsequent form deployments.
  • Lead notification emails went to the brokers (Jayden, Nathan, Joshua), not to Simon (the QA/PM consultant). An email reading "Dear Mdaniels.Mmd@Gmail.Com" is easily dismissed as spam or a one-off by busy brokers processing hundreds of leads per month.
